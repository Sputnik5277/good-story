# Framework Governance

Use this reference when imported writing frameworks might conflict. The goal is not to maximize drama; the goal is to produce a better paper by organizing true evidence into a clear, compelling, defensible argument.

## Supreme Rule

The best story is the strongest honest story that would survive expert review.

Do not use storytelling to:

- Hide inconvenient evidence.
- Inflate a narrow finding into a broad conclusion.
- Convert correlation into causality.
- Treat surrogate outcomes as final outcomes.
- Imply priority or novelty that the literature does not support.
- Smooth away uncertainty that readers need to judge the work.

Use storytelling to:

- Select the central claim.
- Sequence evidence by inference.
- Make the gap and stakes visible.
- Calibrate claims to data.
- Help readers remember what changed.

## Priority Stack

If frameworks disagree, apply them in this order:

1. Transparent reporting: include the information needed to evaluate the work.
2. Evidence integrity: represent all story-relevant results, including negative or limiting results.
3. Claim logic: connect claim, evidence, warrant, qualifier, and rebuttal.
4. Research space: show the field territory, the niche, and the contribution.
5. Story arc: arrange context, tension, action, and resolution.
6. Style: polish language, flow, emphasis, and memorability.

The rule: a lower layer can make a higher layer clearer, but cannot make it less true.

## Framework Concordance

| Framework | Use it for | What it must not do |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Schimel OCAR | Opening, Challenge, Action, Resolution across paper/paragraph scales | Create a false resolution when the data are conditional |
| Olson ABT | One-sentence narrative tension: And, But, Therefore | Manufacture conflict or oversimplify multi-factor evidence |
| Swales CARS | Introduction logic: territory, niche, occupy niche | Make the gap sound larger than it is |
| Mensh/Kording C-C-C | Recursive structure for paper, section, paragraph, figure | Become formulaic if the field expects another order |
| Toulmin | Claim, grounds, warrant, qualifier, rebuttal | Let the claim outrun the grounds |
| Craft of Research | So-what, problem, claim, reasons, evidence, objections | Turn every paper into a grand thesis |
| Heilmeier Catechism | Proposal-style stakes, novelty, risk, tests of success | Overpromise feasibility or impact |
| EQUATOR/reporting guidelines | Complete and transparent reporting | Become a substitute for story or argument |
| Anti-spin guidance | Caveats, limits, no hype, no boasting | Drain the paper of warranted significance |

## Common Conflict Resolutions

- ABT wants a dramatic "But", but the real gap is incremental: state the precise bottleneck or uncertainty; do not pretend the field is confused if it is simply incomplete.
- OCAR wants a Resolution, but the data are mixed: make the resolution a boundary condition, revised model, or sharpened uncertainty.
- CARS wants a niche, but the literature is broad: define the niche by audience, scale, method, system, mechanism, or decision need.
- A high-impact framing wants broad relevance, but the evidence is narrow: use a narrow claim plus a carefully worded implication.
- A clean results story omits a negative result that affects interpretation: include it in the main evidence map, even if briefly, and make it part of the scope.
- Methods/reporting requirements feel like they slow the story: keep the main text inferentially clean, but provide enough methods, checks, and supplementary detail for evaluation.

## Anti-Hype Checklist

Before accepting a story, answer:

- What exact claim is supported?
- What evidence supports it?
- What warrant connects the evidence to the claim?
- What qualifier belongs on the claim?
- What alternative explanation would a tough reviewer raise?
- Which negative, null, or limiting result changes the interpretation?
- Which word would be too strong: proves, demonstrates, reveals, universal, first, mechanism, causal, transformative?
- What is the strongest honest sentence, after those checks?

## Better Paper Standard

A better paper is not merely more exciting. It is easier to evaluate, easier to remember, harder to misread, and more useful to the field.
