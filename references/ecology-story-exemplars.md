# Ecology Story Exemplars

Use this reference when extracting or teaching scientific stories from ecology papers. It is a calibration set, not a literature review. Each example highlights the reusable story move and the evidence boundary.

Crossref cited-by counts below were checked on 2026-06-02 and should be treated as approximate indicators, not rankings.

## Quick Taxonomy

| Paper type | Story lesson |
| --- | --- |
| Classic strong-problem paper | A big ecological question becomes conceptually tractable. |
| Field experiment paper | A broad belief becomes a testable causal claim. |
| Consensus/synthesis paper | A fragmented field becomes an organized knowledge map. |
| Global-change paper | Local ecological evidence is scaled into planetary consequence. |
| Small-clever-turn paper | A simple contrast changes how the field interprets a process. |

## Classic Problem Power

### Paine 1966 - Food Web Complexity and Species Diversity

Source: Paine, R. T. 1966. *The American Naturalist*. DOI: 10.1086/282400. Crossref cited-by: about 3524.

- Story type: small-clever-turn / keystone interaction.
- Core story: community diversity is not only a property of how many species are present; it can be controlled by interaction structure and predation.
- Story move to learn: a simple ecological relationship can revise how the field thinks about complexity.
- Evidence boundary: do not turn this into "all ecosystems are predator-controlled"; the lesson is about interaction strength and community regulation.
- Retellable sentence: a food web can be organized by a few interactions whose removal changes the whole community.

### Odum 1969 - The Strategy of Ecosystem Development

Source: Odum, E. P. 1969. *Science*. DOI: 10.1126/science.164.3877.262. Crossref cited-by: about 3933.

- Story type: conceptual synthesis.
- Core story: ecosystem succession can be read as a coordinated shift in structure, metabolism, nutrient cycling, and stability.
- Story move to learn: a field becomes navigable when many observations are organized into one developmental model.
- Evidence boundary: synthesis papers can be powerful without settling every mechanism; avoid presenting the model as universally predictive.
- Retellable sentence: ecosystems have developmental strategies, not just lists of changing species.

## Biodiversity And Ecosystem Function

### Tilman, Wedin, and Knops 1996 - Productivity and sustainability influenced by biodiversity in grassland ecosystems

Source: *Nature*. DOI: 10.1038/379718a0. Crossref cited-by: about 2037.

- Story type: field experiment turning belief into evidence.
- Core story: plant diversity is not only a conservation value; in grassland experiments it affects productivity and nitrogen retention.
- Story move to learn: take a broad ecological belief and make it testable with a clean experimental system.
- Evidence boundary: grassland evidence should not be written as universal ecosystem law without qualifiers.
- Retellable sentence: losing species can mean losing ecosystem function.

### Hooper et al. 2005 - Effects of biodiversity on ecosystem functioning: a consensus of current knowledge

Source: *Ecological Monographs*. DOI: 10.1890/04-0922. Crossref cited-by: about 6130.

- Story type: consensus map.
- Core story: a contentious and fast-growing field can be stabilized by separating what is well supported from what remains unresolved.
- Story move to learn: when the literature is noisy, the story can be a disciplined map of certainty and uncertainty.
- Evidence boundary: consensus does not mean all mechanisms or all contexts are settled.
- Retellable sentence: biodiversity-ecosystem-function research matured when the field could say what was known, unknown, and conditional.

### Cardinale et al. 2012 - Biodiversity loss and its impact on humanity

Source: *Nature*. DOI: 10.1038/nature11148. Crossref cited-by: about 6034.

- Story type: synthesis with societal stakes.
- Core story: biodiversity loss is not an isolated conservation issue; it affects ecosystem services and therefore human well-being.
- Story move to learn: connect an ecological mechanism to a human consequence without pretending all services respond identically.
- Evidence boundary: broad impact language must preserve variation among services, ecosystems, and evidence strengths.
- Retellable sentence: biodiversity loss matters because it changes what ecosystems provide to people.

## Trophic And Defaunation Stories

### Estes et al. 2011 - Trophic Downgrading of Planet Earth

Source: *Science*. DOI: 10.1126/science.1205106. Crossref cited-by: about 3440.

- Story type: global synthesis from interaction logic.
- Core story: losing large consumers does not merely remove charismatic species; it downgrades trophic interactions and cascades through ecosystems.
- Story move to learn: name a process so readers can see many scattered examples as one planetary phenomenon.
- Evidence boundary: not every ecosystem has the same cascade strength; the story is strongest when interaction pathways are explicit.
- Retellable sentence: removing top consumers rewires ecosystems from the top down.

### Dirzo et al. 2014 - Defaunation in the Anthropocene

Source: *Science*. DOI: 10.1126/science.1251817. Crossref cited-by: about 3473.

- Story type: naming a hidden crisis.
- Core story: the Anthropocene is not only about habitat loss or extinction counts; it is also about the functional emptying of animal life.
- Story move to learn: a powerful term can make a diffuse decline legible, but it must be backed by evidence.
- Evidence boundary: "defaunation" covers different taxa, mechanisms, and data qualities; do not flatten all declines into one causal story.
- Retellable sentence: ecosystems can look present while animal functions are disappearing.

## Modern Global-Change Framing

### Newbold et al. 2015 - Global effects of land use on local terrestrial biodiversity

Source: *Nature*. DOI: 10.1038/nature14324. Crossref cited-by: about 3665.

- Story type: big-data pressure estimate.
- Core story: land use translates global change into local biodiversity loss across terrestrial assemblages.
- Story move to learn: use scale and synthesis to make a local ecological pattern policy-relevant.
- Evidence boundary: association across datasets is not the same as a single controlled mechanism; preserve uncertainty about context and causality.
- Retellable sentence: land-use change is a global pressure visible in local community composition.

### Diaz et al. 2019 - Pervasive human-driven decline of life on Earth points to the need for transformative change

Source: *Science*. DOI: 10.1126/science.aax3100. Crossref cited-by: about 2137.

- Story type: assessment-to-action synthesis.
- Core story: biodiversity decline is broad enough that incremental responses may not match the scale of the problem.
- Story move to learn: a synthesis can move from evidence to response when the logic from status to consequence to action is explicit.
- Evidence boundary: action language must not blur the distinction between scientific assessment and policy preference.
- Retellable sentence: the scale of biodiversity decline changes the scale of response required.

## How To Use These Exemplars

When analyzing an ecology paper, classify it first:

1. Is it a classic problem paper, a modern framing paper, a consensus paper, a field experiment, or a global synthesis?
2. What is the protagonist: species richness, trophic interaction, land use, ecosystem function, defaunation, or another process?
3. What is the antagonist: uncertainty, controversy, missing mechanism, scale mismatch, or management need?
4. What is the key turning evidence?
5. What claim level does the evidence support?
6. What should not be copied because it would overclaim in a new manuscript?

## Ecology-Specific Story Warnings

- Do not turn one ecosystem into all ecosystems.
- Do not turn correlation into mechanism.
- Do not treat biodiversity as a single variable when functional identity, evenness, traits, or trophic position matter.
- Do not confuse conservation importance with causal evidence.
- Do not let planetary framing erase local heterogeneity.
- Do not hide weak effect sizes behind large datasets.

The best ecology story usually makes a scale move: from plot to principle, from interaction to ecosystem, from local change to global pressure, or from biodiversity pattern to functional consequence.
