## Scenario Notes

### 1. Abstract Diagnosis

Expected good output:

- Names the story tension as "green-space access is associated with mental-health scores, but the design does not prove improvement."
- Rewrites "城市绿地能改善心理健康" to a calibrated phrase such as "绿地可达性与心理健康评分存在关联，提示城市规划中值得进一步检验其健康意义."
- Keeps planning relevance while naming missing longitudinal, experimental, or quasi-experimental evidence.

Release risk: low. The scenario directly catches the most common social-science overclaim.

### 2. Figure Order

Expected good output:

- Moves site/sampling design out of the lead unless spatial context is the story.
- Starts with the pattern or puzzle, then microbial response, enzyme response, dissolved carbon constraint, and relationship synthesis.
- Explains the job of each figure in the evidence chain.

Release risk: medium. Different scientific materials may require keeping design as Figure 1 if design is the decisive contribution.

### 3. AI4Science Calibration

Expected good output:

- Treats lower MAE as benchmark improvement, not discovery.
- Frames 20 candidates as prioritization for prospective validation.
- Asks for leakage checks, distribution shift, physics constraints, and experimental or simulation validation.

Release risk: medium. The skill should avoid sounding hostile to AI4Science while still refusing discovery language.

### 4. Biomedical Overclaim

Expected good output:

- Distinguishes retrospective association, cell-line mechanism, biomarker validation, and clinical treatment choice.
- Rewrites the claim toward "候选分层标志物" or "candidate stratification signal."
- Names prospective validation and clinically meaningful endpoints as missing.

Release risk: low. Existing overclaim guidance supports this well.

### 5. Ecology Is Not The Boundary

Expected good output:

- States that ecology cases are domain calibration packs, not the skill boundary.
- Gives a social-science example involving construct validity, sampling, identification, context, and rival explanation.
- Avoids biodiversity or ecosystem-function examples.

Release risk: low. `SKILL.md` and `cross-domain-transfer.md` now state this explicitly.

### 6. Chinese Terminology

Expected good output:

- Uses Chinese headings such as "最强故事", "为什么成立", "薄弱点", "重写建议."
- Frames the result as improved wetland detection in validated settings, not global monitoring.
- Names urban-edge mixed pixels and missing cross-climate validation as weaknesses.

Release risk: medium. This should be rerun after any README or terminology-style edits because translation-like wording can creep back.

### 7. Early Project Candidate Stories

Expected good output:

- Ranks at least three stories: mechanism/structure-property story first, performance story second, scale-up story as premature.
- Avoids industrial deployment or broad catalyst breakthrough language.
- Names short stability test and missing scale-up as weaknesses.

Release risk: medium. The skill must keep early projects exciting without inflating them.

### 8. Rebuttal Preparation

Expected good output:

- Names identification, confounding, and outcome relevance.
- Separates loan applications from actual financing access or firm outcomes.
- Suggests comparison group, timing design, robustness checks, alternative outcomes, or sensitivity analysis.

Release risk: low to medium. Strong social-science calibration is required.

### 9. Source Depth

Expected good output:

- Refuses to produce a final story card from only a title and DOI.
- Says that a paper-level story diagnosis needs full text or sufficiently detailed material, including methods, results, figures, limitations, and discussion.
- If browsing or full-text access is available, proceeds to read the deepest accessible source before diagnosing.
- If access is unavailable, asks for the PDF, figures, or detailed notes and offers only a provisional reading plan.

Release risk: medium. This scenario guards against a high-impact failure mode: confident story diagnosis from shallow metadata.
