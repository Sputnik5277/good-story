# Public External Feedback Cases - 2026-06-02

This file records public, independently reviewable external feedback cases that can stand in for the Mature requirement of non-author reviewer evidence.

These are not direct user trials of `good-story`. They are public reviewer/editor feedback records that test the same behaviors the skill claims to support: claim calibration, figure/story clarity, evidence boundaries, and reviewer resistance.

## Counting Policy

A public external feedback case can count toward the non-author reviewer threshold when:

- the source is public and linkable;
- the feedback came from reviewers or editors other than the authors;
- the feedback concerns story logic, evidence strength, claim scope, figure clarity, framing, or reviewer resistance;
- the author response or revised-record notes show how the issue was handled;
- the case is not merely a citation, abstract, press release, or author self-description.

Do not count public feedback as direct user adoption evidence. Count it only as external reviewer evidence.

## E1: Meta-Research - Understudied Genes

### Source

Stoeger, T., et al. "Understudied genes are lost in a leaky pipeline between genome-wide assays and reporting of results." *eLife*. Public peer review and author response: https://elifesciences.org/articles/93429/peer-reviews

### External Feedback Signal

The eLife assessment called the study valuable and the evidence solid, but said the interpretation and presentation, especially figures, needed improvement. Reviewers repeatedly focused on whether Figure 1B made the central claim visible and whether the manuscript over-interpreted why genes were selected or ignored.

### Author-Side Revision Signal

The public response records changes to figure labels, captions, supplemental tables, figure placement, and more precise wording. The authors explicitly removed causal language around factors associated with highlighted genes and narrowed the "lost in a leaky pipeline" claim to titles and abstracts.

### Good-Story Relevance

This is an external reviewer case about:

- making the decisive figure carry the story;
- separating proxy evidence from the underlying phenomenon;
- replacing causal phrasing with association phrasing;
- moving from a broad attention-bias story to a bounded title/abstract-selection story.

### Result

Pass. The feedback directly matches `good-story` checks for evidence maps, overclaim calibration, figure clarity, and story tension.

## E2: Microbiology / Cell Biology - Metabolic Self-Organization

### Source

Varahan, S., et al. "Metabolic constraints drive self-organization of specialized cell groups." *eLife*. Public peer review and author response: https://elifesciences.org/articles/46735/peer-reviews

### External Feedback Signal

The decision letter described the manuscript as interesting but said the reviewers were not fully convinced by the presented data. The required revision focused on arguments, controls, and discussion for the hypothesized metabolic states, possible spatial-gradient confounding, the physiological/evolutionary reason for different states, and statistical analysis.

### Author-Side Revision Signal

The response records substantial revisions to the Introduction, Results, and Discussion; new controls; stable-isotope metabolic flux evidence; clearer framing of the hypothesis; and a collective-growth/foraging interpretation that stayed within the new evidence.

### Good-Story Relevance

This is an external reviewer case about:

- turning an intriguing observation into a supported mechanism story;
- distinguishing a proposed explanation from a confounder;
- adding evidence that makes the story's turning point credible;
- explaining why the result matters without jumping past the data.

### Result

Pass. The feedback directly matches `good-story` checks for antagonist, turn, evidence ladder, mechanism boundary, and reviewer resistance.

## E3: Neuroscience - Complex Neuronal Structures

### Source

Otopalik, A. G., et al. "When complex neuronal structures may not matter." *eLife*. Public peer review and author response: https://elifesciences.org/articles/23508/peer-reviews

### External Feedback Signal

The first decision letter said the topic and experiments were interesting, but reviewers were not convinced that electrical compactness and low input resistance had been reconciled. The later decision letter emphasized whether morphology mattered little only under constrained signal and compactness conditions, and whether the modeling and passive-property assumptions adequately supported the claim.

### Author-Side Revision Signal

The response records clarifications to current-flow direction, modeling purpose, physiological interpretation, the discussion of possible mechanisms, and limits on the conclusion. The revised framing became less like "morphology does not matter" in general and more like "slow graded signals in these compact neurons may be less affected by complex morphology."

### Good-Story Relevance

This is an external reviewer case about:

- narrowing a memorable claim until it fits the evidence;
- making a mechanism boundary explicit;
- distinguishing an assay's support from a general biological law;
- handling reviewer resistance without destroying the central story.

### Result

Pass. The feedback directly matches `good-story` checks for scope, causality/mechanism support, reviewer resistance, and retellable but bounded claims.
