# FAQ

## Is this just polishing?

No. Polishing improves sentences. `good-story` first checks whether the story itself can stand: tension, evidence, claim boundary, reviewer resistance, and field consequence.

## Do I need a complete manuscript?

No. You can bring a result list, figure list, abstract, outline, proposal aim, discussion paragraph, cover letter, or rebuttal draft.

## What if my field is not listed?

Use `templates/field-calibration.md`. The listed fields are examples. The skill asks what your field counts as evidence, how strong your claim can be, and what reviewers will attack.

## Will it make my paper sound high impact?

Only if the evidence supports that scale of claim. If the strongest honest story is narrower, negative, conditional, or methodological, it should say so.

## Can it help with grants?

Yes. Use it to clarify the need, gap, approach, expected evidence, risk, and field consequence. The story still must not promise more than the plan can test.

## Can it help with Chinese writing?

Yes. Chinese answers should use natural Chinese headings and avoid hybrid labels like `Best story`, `Story spine`, `Evidence map`, or `overclaim`.

## What should I provide for the best result?

Provide:

- field;
- target journal or reader;
- available results or figures;
- intended claim;
- weakest point;
- negative results or limitations;
- what you want rewritten.

## What are common failure modes?

- The story follows lab chronology instead of reader logic.
- The abstract says "important" but does not define a real gap.
- Figures list results without moving belief.
- The claim turns association into causation.
- A domain example is copied into the wrong field.
- The output hides limitations to sound stronger.
